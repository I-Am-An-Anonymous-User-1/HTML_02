Minecraft Java Edition Performance

Minecraft Java Edition was, and still is, one of the most horribly optimized games. Servers, like 2Builders2Tools (2B2T, the oldest anarchy server in all of Minecraft), run on some of the best available hardware and still have many measures to prevent lag, yet, they still have issues. There are 2 main reasons for this:

1. Minecraft's code is just not very efficient

I do not know how to code in the Java programming language, let alone an expert at it, so I cannot understand the code myself. But there are plenty of mods that make the game less laggy, with notable examples such as Sodium, Lithium, Phosphor and Optifine for old versions of Minecraft, Entity Culling, FerriteCore, Dynamic FPS, and plenty more! I cannot believe that we have reached the point where Minecraft is so horribly optimized there's about 5 or 6 very well known mods just for optimization! Mojang, fix your game!

2. Minecraft can only take advantage of 1 CPU thread

Now, I'm no expert in this topic and am writing this based on the information I found from others. Most of the numbers in my examples are assumptios just for demonstration purposes, and so are the examples themselves. I will cite my sources, but I have to re-find them, which I will do later. Anyway, here are the basics:

Minecraft updates a world 20 times a second. This is known as the tick rate. When a tick happens, Minecraft needs to do a bunch of checks and make updates to the world, like is that block you just placed a wet sponge? It is. Are you in the nether? Yes. Since both of those are true, it schedules the water to evaporate from the wet sponge on the next tick, turning it back into a regular sponge. There are a LOT of these checks... tens of thousands. Lets assume it does 50,000 per tick. Now, your computer has a CPU, which has multiple threads. Lets assume it has 8, and lets assume it takes 1 microsecond to do 1 calculation. Minecraft does all of it's calculations in 1 thread, except for some edge cases like when stained glass is placed on top of a beacon, but lets ignore those to keep things simple. Since we assume Minecraft does 50,000 calculations every tick, and it takes 1 microsecond to do 1 calculation, it would take about 50 milliseconds to do those calculations. That seems pretty fast, but for big Minecraft servers, since there are multiple players, those calculations can add up and cause lag. A LOT of it. Lets assue it needs to do 1 million calculations now. That would take the server a full second to update the world once, and remember, it's supposed to update the server 20 times a second, which means, it would be 20 times slower than what it should be. And keep in mind, on anarchy servers, people often build lag machines. This makes hosting Minecraft servers very hard because very VERY expensive equipment is needed, potentially in the tens, hundreds, or, for really big servers like Hypixel, even MILLIONS of US Dollars!! But if Minecraft was coded to take advantage of all threads evenly and efficiently, servers would invest in equipment that has potentially hundreds of threads, which means those millions of calculations that took an entire second before can now be done in milliseconds, potentially microseconds, eliminating lag (for the most part because there are always edge cases) and allowing us to have servers that can potentially have thousands of players on at the same time.

You might be thinking, maybe there's some mod that will save the day and allow Minecraft to take advantage of multiple threads. There are 2 likely possibilities: no such mod exists because Minecraft has hundreds of thousands of lines of code and many different versions, or that magical mod does exist, but has so many drawbacks that nearly no one uses it. The only people who can fix this is Mojang Studios, buuuuuuut they have shown in many ways that they do not care about the Minecraft community in a lot of these scenarios. Like in the Account Migrations and The Minecraft Lawsuit sections down below.
